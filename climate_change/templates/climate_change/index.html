Climate Change: Earth Surface Temperature Data

Climate Change: Earth Surface Temperature Data is a collection of data that provides insights into global temperature trends over the last century. It is a comprehensive database that includes monthly average temperatures from over 7,000 weather stations across the globe, covering a period of more than 100 years.

The data is collected and compiled by the Berkeley Earth research group, a non-profit organization established in 2010, whose aim is to provide an independent and transparent analysis of the Earth's climate. The database contains information from 85 different countries, including both developed and developing nations, and covers a period of 60 months.

This comprehensive database provides a valuable resource for scientists and researchers studying climate change and its effects on the Earth's environment. The data allows for the analysis of global temperature trends and can be used to identify patterns and make predictions about future climate changes.

The Climate Change: Earth Surface Temperature Data is freely available to the public and is frequently updated with new information, making it a valuable tool for policymakers, environmental activists, and anyone interested in understanding the effects of human activities on the Earth's climate.
